\bf{Problem 8.9}
\\
\\ \text{Suppose that} $X$ \text {is approximately} $\chi^{2}(m)$ \text{and} $S = X+Y$ \text {is approximately} $ \chi^{2}(m + n)$ \text {and X and Y are independent. Use MGFs to show that} $S-X$ \text {is approximately} $\chi^{2}(n)$
\\
\\ \[ M_{x}(t) = (1-2t)^{\dfrac{-m}{2}}\]
\\ \[ M_{s}(t) = (1-2t)^{\dfrac{-(m+n)}{2}} \]
\\ \[ M_{s}(t) = M_{x}(t)M_{y}(t) \]
\\ \[ M_{y}(t) = \dfrac{M_{s}(t)}{M_{x}(t)} = \dfrac{(1-2t)^{\dfrac{-(m+n)}{2}}}{(1-2t)^{\dfrac{-m}{2}}} = (1-2t)^{\dfrac{-n}{2}} \]
\\ \text {which is the MGF of} $\chi^{2}(n)$
\\ \text {Thus} $ Y=S-X $ \text {is approximately} $\chi^{2}(n)
